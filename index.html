William shakespeare's Sonnets consists of 154 poems, each fourteen lines long (three Quatrain followed by a rhyming Couplet). Sonnet 18 is perhaps the most famous, and begins like this :

Shall I compare three to a summer;s day?
Thou art mote lovely and more temperate.
Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May,
And summer's lease hath all too short a date.
